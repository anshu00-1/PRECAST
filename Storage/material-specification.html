MATERIAL SPECIFICATION:

General Material Specifications: Common types of water tank based on their usage are,

Concrete:

Concrete M40 is a high-strength concrete mix with a characteristic compressive strength of 40 megapascals (MPa), commonly used in construction projects requiring robust and durable concrete structures.

Steel:

Steel Fe500 refers to a high-strength grade of structural steel with a minimum yield strength of 500 megapascals (MPa), commonly utilized in construction for its durability and load-bearing capabilities.

Strands:

12.7 mm Low Relaxation Prestressed Concrete Steel Strands (LRPC) Class II. Stands are used in Custom Factory Assembled Tanks and Custom On-site Assembled Tanks only for post tensioning the walls as per the design requirements and jointing the wall panels.

Jointing the Panels:

Special EPOXYs are used for jointing the Panels

Water Proofing:

Inner Water Proof Coating done using a mixture of Nitto Bond, Cement and Polymers. On outer surface a damp proof coating is done.
